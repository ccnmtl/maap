Module 1: Black Community and Culture

Understandings:

Students will understand that

  • African American communities have been shaped by both external and internal forces.
  • While the existence of slavery limited communal choices of African Americans in early America, African Americans also proactively developed communal structures and cultural forms to meet self-defined needs.
  • African American communal life and cultural expression represented and responded to a set of shared experiences as well as a diversity of viewpoints.
  • External forces, internal visions, and the interaction between external and internal perceptions of its members define a community.
  • African American communities were defined and continue to be defined by the shared experience of exclusion from and oppression within American society; by the common concerns and diversity of viewpoints articulated by its members; and by the interaction between external and internal forces.)

Essential Questions:

  • How did the existence of slavery shape African American communal life and cultural expression?
  • How is a community defined?
  • How was the African American community defined?
